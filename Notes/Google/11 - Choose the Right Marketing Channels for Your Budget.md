## 1. Paid Ads and Your Brand

### Defining Goals

| Goal | Example |
|------|---------|
| **Awareness** | Get brand name out (e.g. social ads, video ads) |
| **Interest** | Tell people about new products or sales |
| **Retargeting** | Bring visitors back who didn't convert |

Multiple goals need multiple campaigns. Use [[02 - Build Your Digital Marketing Strategy]] and [[03 - Know Your Digital Customers]] to align.

### Sales Funnel Stages

| Stage | Focus |
|------|-------|
| **Awareness** | Strong first impression; what makes you stand out; broad audience |
| **Interest** | Shape opinions; highlight advantages; narrow to site visitors, followers |
| **Consideration** | Target considerers; testimonials; site visitors, followers |
| **Conversion** | Offers, discounts, free trials; target interested but not yet converted |

## 2. Search Engine Marketing (SEM)

### Organic vs Paid

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Organic** | Algorithm-chosen; no payment. See [[07 - Help Customers Find You Through Search]]. |
| **Paid** | "Sponsored" at top; you bid on keywords |

### Why SEM Works

| Factor | Benefit |
|--------|---------|
| **Pay per click (PPC)** | Pay only when someone clicks |
| **Targeted** | Searchers are actively looking; keywords match intent |
| **Measurable** | Track every click, conversion, and cost |

### The SEM Auction

Limited ad slots per page. Advertisers bid. Highest ad rank wins better placement. **Ad rank** = Max CPC × Quality Score (+ extensions, UX factors).

### Quality Score (1–10)

| Component | Focus |
|-----------|-------|
| **Expected CTR** | Likelihood of click when shown |
| **Ad relevance** | Match to search intent |
| **Landing page UX** | Usable, useful, fast, clear CTA |

Higher score → better placement, lower cost.

### Keyword Match Types (Google Ads)

| Type | Syntax | Reach |
|------|--------|-------|
| **Broad** | keyword | Related searches; widest reach; AI-optimised matching |
| **Phrase** | "quotes" | Searches including the phrase meaning |
| **Exact** | [brackets] | Same meaning/intent; most control |
| **Negative** | -keyword | Exclude unwanted searches |

### Making Ads Stand Out

Compelling headlines, engaging copy, clear CTAs. **Responsive Search Ads (RSAs):** Provide multiple headlines and descriptions; Google's AI tests combinations and optimises for performance.

### Conversions

**What counts:** Order, enquiry form, PDF download, rate request. **Tracking:** Set up conversion actions in Google Ads; use Google Tag Manager for implementation.

**Value of click** = Conversion rate × Value per conversion. E.g. 15% rate × £40 sale = £6 per click.

## 3. Display Advertising

### SEM vs Display

| Aspect | SEM | Display |
|--------|-----|---------|
| **Targeting** | Active searchers | Browsing behaviour, interests, demographics |
| **Placement** | Search results only | Millions of websites across ad networks |
| **Format** | Text-based RSAs | Banners, video, animations, native |

### How Display Works

| Method | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Ad networks** | Reach many sites at once (Google Display Network covers 90%+ of internet users) |
| **Programmatic** | Automated, AI-driven buying across multiple networks in real-time |

### Targeting

| Option | Example |
|--------|---------|
| **Contextual** | Fitness ads on workout sites |
| **Audience** | Interests (health, wellness) across sites |
| **First-party data** | Your own customer data for targeting (essential post-cookie deprecation) |
| **Similar / lookalike audiences** | AI finds users similar to your best customers |

### Ad Formats

| Format | Use |
|--------|-----|
| **Responsive display ads** | Google's AI adjusts size, format, and appearance to fit any ad space |
| **Video** | Motion and sound; high engagement |
| **Native** | Blends with site content; less intrusive |
| **Interactive** | Quiz, poll, expandable |

## 4. Retargeting

This is the consolidated reference for retargeting across all channels. Module 09 (E-commerce) links here.

### How It Works

Track behaviour on your site; show targeted ads after visitors leave. Define audience (e.g. added to cart but didn't purchase; viewed pricing page but didn't sign up). Create focused ads with relevant offers.

| Step | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Define audience segments** | Cart abandoners, product page viewers, pricing page visitors, past customers |
| **Set up tracking** | Google Ads remarketing tag, Meta Pixel, or platform-specific tracking |
| **Create targeted ads** | Personalised messaging based on what they viewed or did |
| **Set frequency caps** | Limit how often ads appear to avoid fatigue (3–5 impressions per week typical) |
| **Test incentives** | Discounts, free shipping, urgency—but don't always discount; test what works |

### Privacy and First-Party Data

| Change | Response |
|--------|----------|
| **Third-party cookie deprecation** | Safari and Firefox already block; Chrome phasing out. Shift strategy accordingly. |
| **Privacy Sandbox (Google)** | Topics API groups users by interest without individual tracking. |
| **Consent requirements** | GDPR, CCPA require explicit consent. Use consent management platforms (CMPs). |
| **First-party data strategy** | Build direct relationships: email, purchases, on-site behaviour. Most durable targeting method. |
| **Server-side tracking** | Reduces reliance on browser cookies; more accurate data collection. GA4, Google Tag Manager Server-Side. |

### Retargeting Channels

| Channel | How |
|---------|-----|
| **Google Ads** | Display remarketing, search remarketing (RLSA), YouTube remarketing |
| **Meta Ads** | Custom audiences from website visitors, app users, customer lists |
| **Email** | Cart abandonment sequences, browse abandonment (see [[13 - Craft Effective Email Campaigns]]) |
| **Social platforms** | Platform-specific retargeting pixels on TikTok, LinkedIn, Pinterest |

## 5. Advertising on Social Media

This is the consolidated reference for paid social. Module 06 covers organic social strategy.

### Benefits

| Benefit | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Targeted reach** | Age, interests, location, behaviours, lookalike audiences |
| **Quick visibility** | Faster than organic alone |
| **Cost control** | Daily/campaign limits; start with any budget |
| **Instant engagement** | Visits, downloads, purchases |
| **AI optimisation** | Platforms automatically optimise delivery to people most likely to take your desired action |

### Targeting Options

Demographics, interests, behaviours, location, custom audiences (from your data), lookalike/similar audiences, AI-powered broad targeting (Meta Advantage+). Respect privacy controls and consent requirements.

### Ad Types

| Type | Use |
|------|-----|
| **Promoted/boosted posts** | Boost high-performing organic content |
| **Image/video ads** | Photos, video, Stories placement |
| **Carousel** | Multiple images/videos; product range |
| **Collection** | Curated products; shop from ad (Instagram, Facebook) |
| **Lead ads** | Forms for contact capture without leaving platform |
| **Shopping ads** | Product catalogue ads with price and image (Instagram, TikTok, Pinterest) |
| **Spark Ads (TikTok)** | Boost creator content as paid ad while keeping organic engagement |

### Platform Ad Managers

| Platform | Tool |
|----------|------|
| **Meta (Facebook/Instagram)** | Meta Ads Manager; Advantage+ for AI-optimised campaigns |
| **TikTok** | TikTok Ads Manager; Smart Performance Campaigns |
| **LinkedIn** | LinkedIn Campaign Manager; ideal for B2B |
| **Pinterest** | Pinterest Ads; strong for discovery and shopping intent |
| **YouTube** | Google Ads (video campaigns) |

## 6. Advertising on Video Platforms

### Video Ad Formats

| Format | Notes |
|--------|-------|
| **Skippable in-stream** | Before/during/after; skip after 5 seconds |
| **Non-skippable in-stream** | Up to 15s; bumper = 6s; guaranteed views |
| **YouTube Shorts ads** | Vertical, full-screen, mobile-first |
| **In-feed (discovery)** | Looks like organic content; thumbnail draws click |
| **Masthead** | Top of homepage; high visibility; premium cost |

### Approach

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| **1. Know audience** | What they watch; which creators; what topics |
| **2. Create ad** | Match platform style. YouTube = polished or educational; TikTok = raw, trend-driven. |
| **3. Choose format** | Match goal: awareness = bumper/non-skip; engagement = skippable; conversion = in-feed |
| **4. Target** | Keywords, topics, placements, audiences, demographics |
| **5. Monitor and optimise** | Track view rate, CTR, conversions. Test variations. |

## 7. AI-Powered Advertising

### How AI Changes Paid Advertising

| Capability | Example |
|------------|---------|
| **Performance Max (Google)** | One campaign across Search, Display, YouTube, Gmail, Maps. AI optimises budget allocation, targeting, and creative in real-time. |
| **Meta Advantage+** | AI-optimised targeting, creative, and placement across Facebook and Instagram. Reduces manual setup. |
| **Smart Bidding** | Google Ads AI adjusts bids per auction based on likelihood of conversion (Target CPA, Target ROAS, Maximise Conversions). |
| **Dynamic Creative** | AI assembles ad variations from your assets and tests what performs best. |
| **AI copywriting** | Google Ads and Meta now generate ad copy suggestions. Review for accuracy and brand voice. |

### Best Practices for AI-Assisted Campaigns

| Practice | Notes |
|----------|-------|
| **Feed quality data** | AI optimisation is only as good as your conversion data. Ensure tracking is accurate. |
| **Give it time** | AI campaigns need a learning period (typically 2–4 weeks). Don't make major changes during this phase. |
| **Provide diverse creative** | More asset variations = more for AI to test and optimise. |
| **Monitor, don't autopilot** | AI handles execution, but humans set strategy, review creative, and ensure brand safety. |

## 8. Checklists

### Goals and Funnel

- [ ] Match one goal to a funnel stage
- [ ] Craft message for that stage
- [ ] Choose ad format
- [ ] Plan targeting (visitors, followers, lookalikes)

### SEM Research

- [ ] Search keywords relevant to your business
- [ ] Note who advertises and their ad quality
- [ ] Compare ad positions and relevance

### SEM Ad Creation

- [ ] Use keyword tools; find relevant keywords by intent
- [ ] Select by relevance, traffic, competition
- [ ] Write multiple headlines and descriptions for RSA testing

### Conversions

- [ ] List 2–3 actions to track
- [ ] Set up conversion actions in Google Ads
- [ ] Implement tracking via Google Tag Manager

### Retargeting

- [ ] Define audience segments (cart abandoners, page viewers, etc.)
- [ ] Set up tracking pixels/tags on your site
- [ ] Create targeted messaging per segment
- [ ] Set frequency caps
- [ ] Review privacy compliance (consent, GDPR, CCPA)

### Social Ads

- [ ] Identify 1–2 platforms for your audience
- [ ] Set up business ad accounts and tracking pixels
- [ ] Define audience targeting (custom, lookalike, interest-based)
- [ ] Create ad variations for A/B testing

### Video Ads

- [ ] Choose format based on goal (awareness, engagement, conversion)
- [ ] Create content tailored to platform style
- [ ] Set targeting and budget
- [ ] Monitor and optimise after learning period
