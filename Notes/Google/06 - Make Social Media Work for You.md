## 1. Introduction to Social Media

Social media connects businesses with customers, builds trust, and grows audiences. Choose platforms based on your goals and audience.

### Types of Social Media Sites

| Type | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Mainstream** | Large audiences; good for brand awareness | Instagram, TikTok, YouTube—showcase products, build visibility |
| **Niche** | Specific interests; highly targeted engagement | Strava for fitness—challenges, tips, event sponsorships |
| **Emerging** | Newer platforms with growing audiences | Threads, BlueSky—early-mover advantage; evolving features |

### Why Social Media Matters

| Benefit | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Join the conversation** | Connect in real time. Respond to tags and mentions to build trust. |
| **Build trust** | User-generated content (UGC) often feels more authentic than ads. |
| **Stay top-of-mind** | Regular posts keep the brand visible and engaged. Algorithm reward consistency. |
| **Learn and improve** | High vs low engagement signals what content works. |

## 2. Platform Guide

| Platform | Strengths | When to Use |
|----------|-----------|-------------|
| **Instagram** | Visuals, Stories, Reels, Shopping | Product promotion; authentic moments; influencer collaborations; social commerce |
| **TikTok** | Short-form video, trends, Shop | Viral trends; behind-the-scenes; younger audiences; creative challenges; live shopping |
| **YouTube** | Long-form, Shorts, ads | Tutorials, reviews; brand stories; SEO value; ads for broader reach |
| **LinkedIn** | B2B, professional networking | Business insights; recruiting; industry thought leadership; B2B lead gen |
| **Facebook** | Community, groups, events, Marketplace | Build local community; run ads; host events; older demographics |
| **Pinterest** | Visual inspiration, discovery, Shopping | Fashion, home, food; themed boards; drive traffic via pins |
| **Threads** | Text-based conversation, Meta integration | Real-time discussion; brand personality; cross-posting from Instagram |
| **BlueSky** | Decentralised, chronological feed | Tech-savvy audiences; authentic, ad-free engagement (for now) |

## 3. Social Strategy and Engagement

### How to Leverage Social Media

| Strategy | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Identify relevant platforms** | Choose where your audience is (e.g. clothing → Instagram/TikTok, tech → LinkedIn/YouTube). |
| **Create engaging profiles** | Include brand story, location, contact, visuals, values. |
| **Build and engage** | Encourage follows with promotions or competitions; promote via email. Check local laws for contests. |
| **Foster community** | Reply to comments, join discussions, share UGC. Be consistent—selective engagement harms trust. |

### Engagement Plan (3 Steps)

| Step | Focus |
|------|-------|
| **1. Engage** | How will you spark conversations? Replies, questions, polls. |
| **2. Plan content** | What resonates? Visuals, tips, stories. |
| **3. Encourage community** | Contests, promotions, events to deepen relationships. |

## 4. Choosing and Setting Up

### Aligning Sites with Strategy

| Factor | Consideration |
|--------|----------------|
| **Business goals** | E.g. selling → platforms with shopping features (Instagram Shopping, TikTok Shop). |
| **Customer personas** | Where does your audience spend time? See [[03 - Know Your Digital Customers]]. |
| **Customer journey** | Use See, Think, Do, Care to match platforms to each stage. |

### Business Accounts

| Aspect | Action |
|--------|--------|
| **Why** | Analytics, ad tools, contact buttons, shopping features. Personal accounts lack these. |
| **Setup** | Use business account type (e.g. Instagram Business, LinkedIn Company). Use business email. |
| **Alignment** | E.g. Instagram Business allows product tagging for shopping. Keep profile updated. |

## 5. See, Think, Do, Care on Social

Match platforms and activities to each stage. The same platform can span multiple stages.

| Stage | Focus | Platform Fit |
|-------|-------|--------------|
| **See** | Awareness, discovery | Ads, influencers; short, snappy, visual content (Reels, TikToks, Shorts) |
| **Think** | Consideration, engagement | Longer content—videos, tutorials; showcase products; carousel posts |
| **Do** | Conversion | Built-in shopping (Instagram Shop, TikTok Shop); view products, reviews, buy in fewer steps |
| **Care** | Loyalty, advocacy | Direct messages, customer stories, ongoing engagement, exclusive community content |

## 6. Management and Growth

### Building a Strong Profile

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Profile type** | Use business account. Separate personal and professional. |
| **Key info** | Location, contact, website. |
| **Visual branding** | Consistent across platforms; adapt style per platform (polished vs casual). |

### Growing Your Presence

| Tactic | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Post regularly** | Mix formats (images, videos, carousels); experiment. Consistency signals reliability to algorithms. |
| **Interact in comments** | Reply to build relationships; be consistent with positive and negative feedback. |
| **Share UGC** | Highlight customer content; builds trust and community. |
| **Mission and USPs** | Use content to show purpose and differentiators. |
| **Customer focus** | Content for their needs; respond quickly to feedback. |
| **Influencers** | Partner with aligned influencers (see [[#7. Influencers]]). |
| **Contests and discounts** | Run giveaways; tag friends. Clear rules; follow local regulations. |

### Content Tips

- Repurpose blog posts or guides into short social snippets and carousels
- Share testimonials, reviews, feedback screenshots
- Use AI for ideas, captions, and scheduling (see [[04 - Create Engaging Content#3. Using AI for Content Creation]])

> **Paid social advertising:** For detailed guidance on social media ads (targeting, ad types, budgets, and analytics), see [[11 - Choose the Right Marketing Channels for Your Budget#5. Advertising on Social Media]].

## 7. Influencers

### Influencer Categories

| Type | Followers | Best For |
|------|-----------|----------|
| **Nano** | 1K–10K | Small budgets; high engagement; authentic |
| **Micro** | 10K–100K | Niche audiences; moderate budget |
| **Macro** | 100K–1M | Mass awareness; diverse audiences; higher budget |
| **Mega/celebrity** | 1M+ | Global campaigns; high visibility; substantial budget |

### Partnership Types

| Type | Description | Benefit |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Paid partnerships** | Influencer creates aligned content | Control over messaging |
| **Sponsored content** | Influencer discusses product; light guidelines | Reach; genuine feel |
| **Product gifting** | Free products for review; no payment | Authentic word-of-mouth; lower cost. Check ad laws. |
| **Affiliate marketing** | Unique links/codes; commission per sale | Track performance; identify top influencers |
| **Brand ambassadorships** | Long-term representation; events, campaigns | Strong loyalty; consistent representation |
| **UGC creators** | Create content in the style of organic posts; may not post on their own profile | Authentic-looking content for your ads; scalable |

### Considerations

| Factor | Notes |
|--------|-------|
| **Cost** | Varies by tier; higher reach = higher cost. Research benchmarks. |
| **Fit** | Inauthentic partnerships damage trust. Align with audience and values. |
| **Control** | Gifting = more freedom; paid = more control. |
| **Regulations** | Varies by region. Disclosure required (#ad, #sponsored). Contracts help. |
| **Review process** | Approve content (captions, tags) before posting. |
| **Research** | Vet influencers before collaborating. Use tools (Modash, CreatorIQ) for audience verification. |

### Tips for Success

- Prioritise high engagement and loyal audiences over follower count
- Set one clear, measurable goal per partnership
- Track with UTM links, promo codes, or affiliate tracking

## 8. Pitfalls and Best Practices

### Common Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Avoid By |
|---------|----------|
| **Over-selling** | Mix sales with valuable content: tips, updates, behind-the-scenes |
| **One-way communication** | Monitor comments; respond; address complaints. Balance AI tools with human touch. |
| **Spreading too thin** | Focus on 2–3 key platforms. Quality over quantity. |
| **Neglecting research** | Understand audience, preferences, behaviours. |
| **Overlooking analytics** | Track what works; refine strategy; optimise timing. |
| **Ignoring platform nuance** | Each platform has its own culture, algorithm, and content style. Tailor accordingly. |

### Best Practices

| Practice | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Consistency** | Regular, varied content. Schedule posts in advance using management tools. |
| **Measure** | Use platform insights; track engagement, shares, views. Refine based on data. |
| **Stay current** | Algorithms and features change frequently. Follow platform blogs and creator communities. |

## 9. Measuring Success

### Reporting vs Social Listening

| Approach | Focus |
|----------|-------|
| **Reporting** | Engagement—likes, comments, shares; performance metrics. |
| **Social listening** | Conversations; opportunities to join; what's trending. Tools: Brandwatch, Sprout Social, Mention. |

### Understanding Data

| Metric | What It Tells You |
|--------|-------------------|
| **Follower growth** | How your audience is expanding |
| **Top-performing posts** | What content resonates |
| **Audience demographics** | Age, location, interests for tailoring content |
| **Reach vs impressions** | How many unique people saw content vs total views |
| **Engagement rate** | Interactions ÷ reach — more meaningful than vanity metrics |

### Tool Types

| Type | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| **Platform insights** | Built-in data from Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, LinkedIn, etc. |
| **Management tools** | One dashboard; schedule; track; compare platforms (Hootsuite, Buffer, Sprout Social, Later) |
| **Listening tools** | Brand mentions, competitors, trends; improve products and content (Brandwatch, Mention) |
| **Web analytics** | What happens after clicks; traffic, conversions, content impact (GA4) |

## 10. Checklists

### Platform Selection Checklist

- [ ] Which social media sites fit your audience's interests and behaviours?
- [ ] How can each site guide them through See, Think, Do, Care?

### Content Plan Checklist

- [ ] How often will you post?
- [ ] What content types? (videos, carousels, Stories, text posts)
- [ ] What themes? (product highlights, UGC, behind-the-scenes, education)
- [ ] Which platforms will you focus on? (pick 2–3)
- [ ] How often will you monitor comments?
- [ ] What tactics for growth? (competitions, influencer collaborations, UGC campaigns)
