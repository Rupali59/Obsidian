# Maturity Model & Assessment

> 6 levels. 10 dimensions. Three assessment paths. Every question with scoring.

---

## The 6 Maturity Levels

| Level | Name | Description | Typical Client |
|-------|------|-------------|----------------|
| **0** | **Absent** | No meaningful digital presence. Maybe a dormant Facebook page. | New business, offline-only, or completely neglected digital. |
| **1** | **Present** | Website exists. Maybe a social account. No strategy, no tracking, no consistency. | "We have a website but it doesn't do anything for us." |
| **2** | **Active** | Posting on social, maybe running some ads. Analytics exist but aren't used. Content is sporadic. | "We're doing stuff but don't know if it's working." |
| **3** | **Strategic** | Documented goals and personas. Consistent content. Tracking ROI. Channels work together. | "We have a plan and it's working, but we're hitting a ceiling." |
| **4** | **Optimised** | Data-driven decisions. A/B testing. Automation. Multi-channel attribution. Clear team roles. | "We're profitable but want to scale efficiently." |
| **5** | **Leading** | AI-integrated. Predictive analytics. Advanced attribution. Scaling markets. | "We want to stay ahead and find the next growth lever." |

### What Each Level Needs From You

| Level | Engagement | Your Role |
|-------|-----------|-----------|
| **0–1** | Full-service build (6–12 months minimum) | You build everything; gradually train their team |
| **2** | 90-day sprint → retainer | You fix and build; they take over day-to-day |
| **3** | Optimisation retainer (ongoing monthly) | You optimise, they execute; you review and steer |
| **4** | Advisory + specialist projects (quarterly reviews) | You advise strategy; execute only specialist work |
| **5** | Fractional CMO / advisory | You attend leadership meetings; set direction |

## The 10 Dimensions

Each dimension is assessed independently. A business might be Level 4 on social media but Level 1 on email — that unevenness is where your value lives.

### India-Specific Adjustments

| Dimension (Global) | India Adjustment |
|-------------------|-----------------|
| Email Marketing | → **WhatsApp + Email** (WhatsApp is primary; 90%+ open rate vs 15–20% email) |
| Paid Advertising | → **Paid Ads + Marketplace Presence** (Amazon, Flipkart, Meesho are ad platforms too) |
| Conversion Infrastructure | → **Transaction Infrastructure** (UPI, Razorpay, Cash on Delivery, EMI) |
| SEO | → **Search + Discovery** (includes JustDial, Practo, Sulekha, Google Maps) |

## Maturity Profiles — Reading the Shape

| Pattern | What It Means | What to Do |
|---------|---------------|------------|
| **Flat low** (all 0–1) | Starting from scratch | Full build. Maximum impact, maximum effort. Start with foundation. |
| **Spiky** (high on 1–2, low on rest) | Over-invested in one channel | Balance the portfolio. Often: good at social, bad at everything else. |
| **Plateau at 2–3** | Busy but not growing | Needs strategy and measurement. Quick wins from fixing what exists. |
| **High with holes** (mostly 3–4, one at 0–1) | Mature business with blind spots | Targeted fixes. Often: great content but no email, or good ads but terrible website. |
| **Uniformly high** (all 4–5) | Doesn't need a generalist | Advisory or specialist engagement. |

## Scoring

**Per dimension:** Average of question scores in that section, scaled to 0–5.

```
Dimension Scaled Score = (Average of question scores / 4) × 5
```

**Overall:** Average of all 10 scaled dimension scores.

| Score Range | Level |
|-------------|-------|
| 0.0 – 0.9 | Level 0: Absent |
| 1.0 – 1.9 | Level 1: Present |
| 2.0 – 2.9 | Level 2: Active |
| 3.0 – 3.9 | Level 3: Strategic |
| 4.0 – 4.5 | Level 4: Optimised |
| 4.6 – 5.0 | Level 5: Leading |

Always show the dimensional breakdown — the overall score hides the shape.

## Gateway Questions (Before Assessment)

Three plain-English questions determine which path the prospect takes:

**G1.** Does your business have a website?
- Yes / No / I'm not sure

**G2.** When someone wants to find your business online, can they?
- Yes — we show up on Google
- Sometimes, but not always
- Probably not
- I've never checked

**G3.** How do most of your customers find you today?
- Word of mouth / referrals
- They walk past / see our sign
- Social media
- Google / online search
- Paid advertising
- I'm not sure

**Routing:**

| If... | Path |
|-------|------|
| No website + probably not findable + word-of-mouth | **Path A: Starting Fresh** (12 questions, plain English, 3 min) |
| Has website + mixed findability + mix of channels | **Path B: Growing** (standard 40 questions, tooltips, 5–7 min) |
| Has website + ranks on Google + multiple channels | **Path C: Scaling** (full technical, 5–7 min) |
