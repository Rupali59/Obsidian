## Part 2: Verification

The assessment asks questions. People overestimate. Level 0 clients genuinely don't know. Verification gives you independent truth.

### The 10-Minute Independent Audit

Needs only: business name + location. No account access.

| # | Check | How | Tool | Time |
|---|-------|-----|------|------|
| 1 | Google business name | "[name] [city]" | Google Search | 30s |
| 2 | Google service + location | "[service] near me" | Google Search | 30s |
| 3 | Google Business Profile | Search on Maps | Google Maps | 1 min |
| 4 | Website (if any) | Visit URL | Browser | 1 min |
| 5 | PageSpeed Insights | pagespeed.web.dev | Google PSI | 1 min |
| 6 | Analytics installed | View source → search "gtag" "analytics" "gtm" | DevTools | 30s |
| 7 | Meta tags | View source → `<title>` and `<meta description>` | DevTools | 30s |
| 8 | Social profiles | Search Instagram, Facebook, YouTube | Social platforms | 2 min |
| 9 | JustDial / Practo / Sulekha | Search category + area | Directory sites | 1 min |
| 10 | WhatsApp presence | Check listed number | WhatsApp | 30s |

|-------------------|----------------|

### When Verification Disagrees with Self-Assessment

| They Say | Verification Shows | Your Move |
|----------|-------------------|-----------|
| "We show up on Google" | They don't rank for what customers search | Show them: "Search '[service] in [area]'. See? Your competitor shows up." |
| "We don't have analytics" | GA4 is installed (someone set it up once) | Good news: data probably exists. Get access. |
| "We're active on social" | Last post was 4 months ago | Show the date. Frame as opportunity, not criticism. |
| "Our website is good" | PageSpeed 35/100 | Show them PageSpeed on their phone. The number talks. |

### Verification-First Flow

For local Indian clients, run the audit **before** they take the assessment:

```
Business name + Location
        │
        ▼
  YOU RUN 10-MIN AUDIT (independent data)
        │
        ▼
  YOU KNOW THEIR LEVEL (before they answer anything)
        │
        ▼
  THEY TAKE ASSESSMENT (their answers add context; you cross-reference)
        │
        ▼
  COMBINED REPORT (verified score + self-assessment = 10x more credible)
```

### Automated Verification (via Tathya Plugin API)

Half of the audit can be automated. See [[04 - Architecture & Implementation]] for the `POST /assessments/:id/verify` endpoint:

| Check | Automatable? | How |
|-------|-------------|-----|
| Google search ranking | Semi | SerpAPI or Google Custom Search API |
| Google Business Profile | Semi | Google Places API / Maps API |
| Website exists + speed | Yes | PageSpeed Insights API |
| Analytics installed | Yes | Server-side fetch, regex for gtag/gtm |
| Meta tags | Yes | Fetch page, parse `<head>` |
| Social profiles | Semi | Graph API (limited) or public page fetch |
| WhatsApp Business | Manual | Check in person |
| JustDial/Practo | Semi | Scrape or API |

## Part 4: Sprint Planning by Maturity Profile

### Foundation Order

Some dimensions are prerequisites. If Layer 1 is below Level 2, Sprint 1 fixes them regardless.

```
LAYER 1 (fix first — everything depends on these):
├── Website & Tech
├── Analytics & Data
└── Strategy & Goals

LAYER 2 (build on working foundation):
├── Search & Discovery
├── Content
├── WhatsApp + Email
└── Transaction Infrastructure

LAYER 3 (amplify what's working):
├── Social Media
├── Paid Advertising
└── Team & Process
```

### Flat Low (All 0–1)

| Sprint | Focus | Actions |
|--------|-------|---------|
| **1** (Days 1–30) | Layer 1 | Build/fix website. Install GA4. 2–3 SMART goals. 1 persona. Claim GBP. Set up WhatsApp Business. Email capture form. |
| **2** (Days 31–60) | Layer 2 | Keyword research. 4 content pieces. Welcome email/WA flow. Optimise core pages. Social content templates. |
| **3** (Days 61–90) | Layer 3 | First paid campaign (small budget). Retargeting. Regular social cadence. First monthly report. Train client team. |

**Target:** Level 0–1 → Level 2. Client has a functioning digital marketing system.

### Spiky (High on 1–2, Low on Rest)

| Sprint | Focus | Actions |
|--------|-------|---------|
| **1** | Fix foundation under the spike | Social strong but analytics 0? Install GA4, track what social drives. Website strong but SEO 0? Keyword research, on-page optimisation. Fix conversion tracking. |
| **2** | Build adjacent dimensions | Use strong channel's audience to build weak ones (social → email: "sign up for exclusive content"). Create content supporting SEO using topics popular on social. |
| **3** | Balance and scale | Paid ads to amplify strong channel. Automate what's built (email flows, social scheduling). Cross-channel reporting. |

**Target:** Even out the profile. Strong channel has measurement, weak channels have structure.

### Plateau at 2–3 (Busy but Stuck)

| Sprint | Focus | Actions |
|--------|-------|---------|
| **1** | Strategy + measurement | Document SMART goals. Fix tracking (GA4 exists but no conversions). Audit content and ads for ROI. Kill what's not working. |
| **2** | Systematise + optimise | Build content calendar. Set up automation (nurture, not blast). A/B test ads. Implement reporting cadence. |
| **3** | Scale + introduce missing | Scale winning ads. Launch retargeting. Introduce weakest dimension (usually email or SEO). Build team processes. |

**Target:** Level 2 → Level 3. Shift from "doing stuff" to "doing stuff that works."

### High with Holes (Mostly 3–4, One at 0–1)

| Sprint | Focus | Actions |
|--------|-------|---------|
| **1** | Patch the holes | Email at 0? Full setup — platform, capture, welcome, first campaign. Analytics low? Proper GA4, dashboards, conversion tracking. |
| **2** | Integrate patched dimensions | Connect new channel to existing. Email list from social. Analytics informing content. Retargeting from new tracking. |
| **3** | Advanced optimisation | A/B testing across channels. AI integration. Advanced automation. Focus on efficiency and scale. |

**Target:** Eliminate gaps. Uneven 3–4 → consistent 3–4 with path to 5.

## Part 8: The Translation Table

Every digital concept has a physical-world equivalent Level 0 clients already understand:

| Digital Concept | Physical Equivalent |
|----------------|-------------------|
| Website | Your shop front that's open 24/7, even when you're asleep |
| SEO | Being in the phone book under the right category, on the first page |
| Google Business Profile | Your sign on the high street — people walking by see your name, hours, reviews |
| Analytics | Counting how many people walk past, come in, and buy |
| Conversion rate | Out of 100 who walk in, how many buy? |
| Email/WhatsApp list | Your book of regular customers' contact details |
| Content marketing | The advice you'd give a customer face-to-face, written so thousands can see it |
| Social media | The town square where your customers chat — join or be invisible |
| Paid ads | A poster where your customers will see it — pay only when someone looks |
| Retargeting | Remembering the person who picked up your product, put it down, and walked out — and reminding them |
| Content calendar | Planning your weekly specials in advance |
| Brand guidelines | Rules that make your shop look the same whether someone visits Monday or Saturday |

### Conversation Framework for Level 0

**Step 1: Ask about their business, not their marketing.**
"Tell me about your best week of business last year. What happened? Now tell me about a slow week. Why was it quiet?"

**Step 2: Connect to digital.**
"You said your best week was when someone mentioned you in that Facebook group. What if 50 people did that every month?"

**Step 3: Show evidence.**
"Right now, if someone new searches '[service] near me,' they find your competitor. Not because they're better. Because they showed up."

**Step 4: Make the first step tiny.**
"I want to make sure when someone searches your business name, they find your address, phone, hours, and best photos. Takes 2 hours. Everything else comes later."

### Metrics That Prove You Right

- GBP views, GBP actions (calls, directions, website clicks)
- Google Maps search queries
- WhatsApp enquiry count
