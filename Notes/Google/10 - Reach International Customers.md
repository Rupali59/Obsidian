## 1. Planning International Expansion

### Key Benefits of Expanding Internationally

| Benefit | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Reach new audiences** | Tap into markets where demand for your product or service is growing. |
| **Boost revenue** | Access larger customer bases to drive sales. |
| **Diversify** | Reduce reliance on a single market by spreading risk across regions. |

### How Digital Marketing Supports Global Expansion

| Channel | Use |
|---------|-----|
| **Social media** | Connect with audiences in new regions. Use analytics for local trends and hashtags; research influencers. Tailor content and run targeted ads. |
| **Targeted reach** | Use Google Ads, Meta Ads to target regions or demographics with high demand. Analyse and optimise regularly. |
| **AI-powered insights** | Tools like Google Trends, GA4 geographic reports, and AI market research can identify high-potential markets before you invest. |

### PESTEL Analysis

PESTEL = Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Environmental, Legal. Use it to assess new markets by examining external factors.

| Factor | Considerations |
|--------|----------------|
| **Political** | Taxation, grants, funding, government initiatives, political stability. |
| **Economic** | Spending power, interest rates, inflation, exchange rates. Adjust pricing by market. |
| **Social** | Demographics, lifestyle trends, attitudes. Research local influencers and brands. |
| **Technological** | Tech infrastructure, logistics, dominant platforms (e.g. WeChat in China, LINE in Japan). |
| **Environmental** | Sustainability in transport and packaging. Transparency can resonate with customers. |
| **Legal** | Laws affecting your product; trading policies, regulations, data protection (GDPR in EU, LGPD in Brazil, etc.). |

### Research Tools

| Tool Type | Use |
|-----------|-----|
| **Google Trends** | Compare search interest across regions and over time. |
| **Google Keyword Planner** | Discover popular search terms and competition by region. |
| **GA4** | Geographic reports show where your visitors are already coming from. |
| **AI research** | Use ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini to summarise market conditions, cultural factors, and competitor landscape for target regions. |

## 2. Adapting for International Audiences

### Translation vs Localisation

| Type | Focus | Example |
|------|-------|---------|
| **Translation** | Converting text accurately between languages. | 'Contact Us' → 'Contactez-nous' |
| **Localisation** | Cultural adaptation beyond word-for-word. | 'Add to Cart' → 'In den Warenkorb' (Germany); adjusting imagery, colours, date formats, currency. |

### AI for Translation

| Strength | Limitation |
|----------|------------|
| **Speed** — large volumes quickly | **Robotic tone** — can sound unnatural or overly formal |
| **Cost** — often cheaper than human translation | **Cultural gaps** — misses humour, slang, regional expressions |
| **Good starting point** — DeepL, Google Translate, ChatGPT | **Context errors** — idioms and metaphors lose meaning |

**Best practice:** Use AI as a starting point; keep native speakers in the loop for review and cultural adaptation.

### SEO for International Audiences

| Action | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Separate pages per language** | Each language has its own URL (e.g. `www.example.com/fr`). |
| **Local information** | Region-specific details; regional holidays; local contact options (avoid international numbers). |
| **Language annotations** | Use `hreflang` tags so search engines serve the correct version. |
| **Local keyword research** | Same product may be searched differently in different markets. Research per region. |

### Common SEO Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| **Mixing languages on one page** | Don't combine multiple languages on the same page. |
| **Ignoring local site speed** | Compress images; use CDN with edge servers near target markets. |
| **Ignoring country codes** | Use country-specific domains (e.g. .co.uk, .fr) or subdirectories where appropriate. |

## 3. Preparing for International Sales

### The International Customer Journey

Adapt processes for global audiences. See [[03 - Know Your Digital Customers]] for the general journey framework.

| Stage | Focus |
|-------|-------|
| **Discovery** | Global SEO; targeted ads tailored to new regions. Local influencer partnerships. |
| **Checkout** | Clear pricing in local currency; transparent tax; preferred payment options. |
| **Post-purchase** | Responsive support; communication aligned with cultural expectations. |

### Payment Systems

| Consideration | Action |
|---------------|--------|
| **Preferred methods** | Research what customers use. Stripe and PayPal support multiple currencies. Region-specific: iDEAL (Netherlands), Bancontact (Belgium), UPI (India), Alipay/WeChat Pay (China). |
| **Refunds** | Support refunds in the customer's currency. Check local consumer protection laws. |

### Legislation and Regulation

| Area | Focus |
|------|-------|
| **Import/export** | Restrictions, permits, quantity limits, prohibited goods. |
| **Tax** | VAT, customs duties, agreements affecting costs. |
| **Data protection** | GDPR (EU/UK), LGPD (Brazil), CCPA (California), PIPL (China). |
| **Consumer protection** | Returns, refunds, warranties. Be transparent. |

## 4. Delivering to Global Customers

### Supply Chain Essentials

| Action | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Inventory** | Review regularly; adequate stock for demand. |
| **Demand** | Monitor patterns; avoid stockouts or overstocking. |
| **Fulfilment** | Process and ship promptly. Consider fulfilment partners (e.g. Amazon FBA, ShipBob) for international markets. |

### Shipping Logistics

| Consideration | Notes |
|---------------|-------|
| **Partners** | Choose reliable carriers with access to target markets and customs expertise. |
| **Costs and time** | Balance delivery time and cost. Offer tracked shipping. |
| **Returns** | Support international returns. Consider pre-paid return labels or local return addresses. |

## 5. Checklists

### Website SEO for International Audiences

- [ ] Create separate pages for each language and region
- [ ] Include localised support details (avoid international phone numbers)
- [ ] Add `hreflang` annotations to each page
- [ ] Optimise images and use CDN for fast loading in target regions
- [ ] Research local keywords for each target market

### International Expansion Readiness

- [ ] Research import/export restrictions
- [ ] Research local tax obligations (e.g. VAT)
- [ ] Verify data protection compliance (GDPR, LGPD, etc.)
- [ ] Review local laws for returns, refunds, and warranties
- [ ] Identify preferred payment methods in target markets
