## 1. Planning Digital Content

### What is Content Marketing?

Content marketing is the creation and sharing of online material (videos, blogs, social media posts) designed to generate interest in a company's products or services by capturing attention.

### How It Supports Business Goals

Increases sales by directing traffic to your website, boosting brand awareness, strengthening brand identity, and building trust and recognition.

### Types of Content

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Blogs** | Published on a website; original or guest-authored. Increases publicity and provides shareable content for other channels. |
| **Infographics** | Visual knowledge presentation. Eye-catching; good for complex content. |
| **Ebooks / guides** | Educational guides on a specific topic. Positions you as an expert. Often used as lead magnets. |
| **Videos** | Demos, tutorials, testimonials, Reels, Shorts, TikToks. Engaging and consumable on the go. See [[14 - Create Engaging Videos for Your Customers]]. |
| **Social media posts** | Product images, videos, reviews, blogs. Boosts visibility, drives traffic, enables in-app shopping. |
| **Podcasts** | Show expertise; inform and entertain. Drives visitors to website and other content. |
| **Newsletters** | Regular email content (or via platforms like Substack, Beehiiv). Builds direct audience relationship. |
| **Interactive content** | Quizzes, calculators, polls, AR try-ons. High engagement and shareability. |

### Purpose of Content

| Purpose | Description | Examples |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| **Entertain** | Engage and grab attention; reflect business values | Quizzes, competitions, amusing posts or videos |
| **Inspire** | Motivational; encourage action | Reviews, personal stories, influencer collaborations |
| **Educate** | Inform or instruct | Blog posts, how-to videos, infographics |
| **Convince** | Persuasive; encourage action | Case studies, offers, charts, interactive demos |

### Planning Your Content

| Step | Focus |
|------|-------|
| **Consider goals** | Match content goal to business goal (e.g. more followers → entertaining, inspiring content) |
| **Consider platform** | Choose platform that supports content and goals (e.g. website purchases → reviews, how-tos, blogs) |
| **Match type and purpose** | Educate → guides, ebooks, infographics; Entertain → quizzes, competitions |
| **Be practical** | Create content you can maintain consistently; consider outside help for specialist formats |
| **Repurpose** | One piece of content → multiple formats. Blog → video script → social carousel → email snippet → podcast talking point. |

## 2. Creating Digital Content

### Creating Content for Your Audience

| Action | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Identify preferences | Learn what content and channels your audience uses |
| Learn about habits | Understand behaviours and routines |
| Keep pain points in mind | What problems does your product solve? |
| Use analytics | Track what works using GA4 and platform insights |
| Keep experimenting | Test and refine |

### Ingredients of Content Success

| Ingredient | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| **Answers questions** | Address your audience's needs |
| **Provides value** | Give something useful |
| **Keeps engagement** | Leave them wanting more |

### Brand Guidelines

Established businesses often have brand guidelines covering:

| Element | Examples |
|---------|----------|
| Brand attributes | Honest, creative, ethical |
| Mission and values | — |
| Tone of voice | Playful, informative |
| Product descriptions | Particular ways to describe offerings |
| Colours | Specific colours allowed |
| Images | Illustrations vs photography |
| Typography | Fonts and sizes |
| AI usage policy | Rules for AI-generated content, disclosure requirements |
| Best practice examples | Content that demonstrates standards |

**Benefits:** Saves time, protects identity, ensures cohesive, professional content customers trust.

### Writing Top Tips

- Write for an online audience
- Grab attention early
- Remember who your customer is
- Don't overcomplicate
- Encourage action
- Write for humans first, then optimise for search and AI discoverability

## 3. Using AI for Content Creation

This is the reference section for AI in content marketing. Other modules reference this section for AI fundamentals.

### What is Generative AI?

Generative AI creates content (text, images, videos, audio) and can provide insights and personalise experiences. You interact via prompts, voice, or images.

### AI Tools for Content Marketing

| Category | Tools | Use |
|----------|-------|-----|
| **Text generation** | ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Microsoft Copilot | Drafts, brainstorming, editing, repurposing |
| **Image generation** | Midjourney, DALL-E, Adobe Firefly, Canva AI | Social graphics, product mockups, illustrations |
| **Video generation** | Runway, Synthesia, CapCut AI, Descript | Short clips, explainers, video editing |
| **Audio / voice** | ElevenLabs, Descript, Podcastle | Podcasts, voiceovers, audio content |
| **Design** | Canva, Figma AI, Adobe Express | Templates, presentations, social assets |
| **Scheduling & management** | Buffer, Hootsuite, Sprout Social, Later | Content calendars, cross-platform publishing |

### Prompting: T-C-R-E-I Framework

**Thoughtfully Create Really Excellent Inputs**

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Task** | What you want the AI to do. Includes **Persona** (e.g. speechwriter, marketing exec with 15 years experience) and **Format** (bullets, table, short sentences). |
| **Context** | Necessary details. E.g. 'Give me five birthday gift ideas. Budget £30. Recipient: 29-year-old who loves winter sports, recently switched from snowboarding to skiing.' |
| **References** | Examples or past outputs to guide the AI. Paste in your brand guidelines, previous successful content, or competitor examples. |
| **Evaluate** | Did the output meet your needs? |
| **Iterate** | Refine prompt with more information or edits until you get what you need. |

### AI Best Practices

| Consideration | Action |
|---------------|--------|
| **Human in the loop** | AI complements human skills; requires human review. Use for support, not replacement. |
| **Be responsible** | Evaluate and verify output. Be conscious of bias, stereotypes, incorrect information (hallucinations). |
| **Stay secure** | Avoid entering confidential or personal data. Check company policies; understand how data is used and retained. |
| **Disclose when required** | Some regions and platforms require labelling AI-generated content. Check local regulations. |
| **Feed your brand voice** | Give AI your brand guidelines, tone examples, and past content so output is consistent with your identity. |

## 4. Ethics and Legal Responsibilities

Content creation is governed by regional legislation and ethical practices. Research what applies in your regions and your customers' regions.

### Ethical Digital Marketing Practices

| Practice | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Be truthful** | Don't misrepresent or mislead about products, services, or business. |
| **Share the credit** | Acknowledge origin of content (images, reviews, AI-generated work); seek permission where needed. |
| **Label AI content** | Disclose AI-generated content where required. Platform policies (Meta, Google, TikTok) increasingly require AI labels. |
| **Make adverts obvious** | Clearly label ads vs impartial reviews; especially when working with influencers (#ad, #sponsored). |
| **Be clear and audience-conscious** | Avoid jargon; don't target vulnerable audiences who may not grasp impact of complex purchases. |
| **Be representative and inclusive** | Reflect diversity—ages, body shapes, abilities, ethnicities, gender, parental status. |

### Legal Concepts for Consideration

| Concept | Notes |
|---------|-------|
| **GDPR** | EU/UK data protection; applies if registered in EU/UK or have EU/UK customers. |
| **Data protection and privacy** | Collection, storage, use of data; email marketing requires permissions. First-party data strategies are now essential as third-party cookies are deprecated. |
| **Discrimination laws** | Accessibility for visually/hearing impaired, motor function limitations. |
| **Brand and trademark** | Mentioning other brands may require permission. |
| **Intellectual property** | Content, images, sounds have usage rights; seek authorisation when uncertain. AI-generated content copyright is still evolving—check jurisdiction. |
| **Online advertising laws** | Representation of offers; not causing offence. Varies by region. |
| **AI-specific regulation** | EU AI Act and other emerging frameworks may affect how AI-generated marketing content is disclosed and used. |

## 5. Creating a Content Calendar

### What is a Content Calendar?

A structured timeline that organises content marketing: what to publish, when, and on which platform. Ensures consistency and efficiency; can boost sales.

### Getting Started

| Step | Focus |
|------|-------|
| **Choose your tool** | Notion, Trello, Asana, Google Sheets, or dedicated tools (Buffer, CoSchedule, Sprout Social). AI can help generate calendar drafts. |
| **Remember your goals** | E.g. +20% website visits → content that regularly drives traffic. |
| **Know your audience** | Segment; specify which group per post. When are they online? Which channels? |
| **Remember your purpose** | Entertain, inspire, educate, convince—match content and platforms. |
| **Identify key moments** | Holidays, festivals, seasonal events, industry events, product launches. |
| **Create your content** | Link posts, images, videos to the calendar. Stays organised; allows review before publish. Batch-create content where possible. |
| **Analyse and iterate** | Track data—posting frequency, content effectiveness, campaign impact on sales and traffic. |

## 6. Checklists

### Planning Content Checklist

- [ ] Revisit business goals from [[02 - Build Your Digital Marketing Strategy]]
- [ ] Which purposes help achieve those goals? (Entertain, inspire, educate, convince)
- [ ] Note 2–3 content types per purpose and which channels to use
- [ ] Plan repurposing: how can one piece become multiple formats?

### Audience Content Checklist

- [ ] What content do customers prefer and where do they access it?
- [ ] What pain points does your product or service solve?
- [ ] How do preferences align with your plan? Make updates as needed

### Brand Attributes Checklist

- [ ] List three words that describe your products/services
- [ ] List three words that summarise your values, mission, and vision
- [ ] List three words that describe your ideal customer
- [ ] Write down attributes you want readers to associate with your brand
- [ ] Document your AI usage policy (what's acceptable, what requires human review)

### AI Prompting Checklist

- [ ] Input Task (persona + format)
- [ ] Add Context (audience, business, tone)
- [ ] Include References (brand guidelines, past content, competitor examples)
- [ ] Evaluate the output for accuracy, tone, and brand alignment
- [ ] Iterate—adjust prompt for better results

### Content Calendar Checklist

- [ ] Select a tool (Notion, Trello, Buffer, or spreadsheet)
- [ ] Remind yourself of goals and audience preferences
- [ ] Choose purpose and topic for first piece
- [ ] Plot on calendar (time, channel); use analytics for best times
- [ ] Plan complementary content; use different channels for same message
- [ ] Plan until satisfied with amount and variety; keep it manageable
