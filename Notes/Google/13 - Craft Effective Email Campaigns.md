## 1. Introduction to Email Marketing

### Why Email Marketing?

Cost-effective way to build loyalty and boost engagement. Owned channel—you control the audience list (unlike social media algorithms). Reaches customers on mobile, tablet, and desktop.

### Set SMART Goals

Use the framework from [[02 - Build Your Digital Marketing Strategy#SMART Goals Framework]]:

| Criterion | Apply to Email |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Specific** | Single purpose, e.g. drive more website visitors |
| **Measurable** | Track opens, clicks, sales (e.g. 100 sales in 2 weeks) |
| **Achievable** | Match list size; 100 emails → 200 sales may be unrealistic |
| **Relevant** | Align with business goals |
| **Time-bound** | Realistic deadlines |

### Building Your Contact List

Ask for email addresses. Examples: sign-up at checkout, in-store, on website (pop-ups, lead magnets, content upgrades), at events. Grow through valuable content and offers. Never buy email lists—it damages deliverability and violates most regulations.

### Subscriber Etiquette

| Practice | Benefit |
|----------|---------|
| **Double opt-in** | Reduces fake signups; ensures valid addresses. Required in some countries (e.g. Germany, Luxembourg, Norway). Check GDPR. |
| **Unsubscribe option** | Legal in many regions; keeps list active; respects preferences. One-click unsubscribe now required by Gmail and Yahoo. |
| **Regular cadence** | Protects sender reputation; avoids spam flags |
| **List hygiene** | Regularly remove hard bounces and long-term inactive subscribers |

### Email Platforms

| Platform | Best For |
|----------|----------|
| **Mailchimp** | Small businesses; ease of use; free tier |
| **Klaviyo** | E-commerce; deep Shopify/WooCommerce integration; AI-powered |
| **Brevo (Sendinblue)** | Budget-friendly; includes SMS and chat |
| **HubSpot** | Full CRM + marketing suite; B2B |
| **ConvertKit (Kit)** | Creators and newsletters |
| **ActiveCampaign** | Advanced automation and segmentation |

### Targeting and Automation

| Approach | Use |
|----------|-----|
| **Welcome series** | Personalise with name; introduce business; quick poll on interests. 3–5 email sequence. |
| **Segmented offers** | Cat owners → cat offers; dog owners → dog offers |
| **Behaviour-triggered** | Cart abandonment, browse abandonment, post-purchase, re-engagement |
| **Seasonal content** | Spring grooming tips; winter coat discounts |

## 2. Creating Email Content

### Crafting Great Emails

| Element | Best Practice |
|---------|---------------|
| **Subject line** | Make them want to open; first impression with "From" and subject. Keep under 50 characters for mobile. |
| **Preview text** | The snippet after the subject line—use it to complement, not repeat, the subject. |
| **Content** | Concise; compelling content at top; short paragraphs; link for more |
| **Tone** | Consistent brand voice; email can be more casual and friendly |
| **Links** | CTAs, "Get 25% off", "Learn more"; logo → homepage; social icons |
| **Design** | Buttons over text links; bold for key offers |

### Personalisation

| Tag | Use |
|-----|-----|
| **[First Name]** | "Hi Jane!" |
| **[City/Region]** | "Exclusive offers for London customers!" |
| **[Product Purchased]** | "How are you finding your flea treatment?" |
| **Dynamic content blocks** | Show different content sections to different segments within the same email |

Ensure personalisation supports GDPR: collect only needed data, explicit consent, allow updates/removal.

### Visual Best Practices

| Rule | Notes |
|------|-------|
| **60:40 text-to-image** | Avoid spam filters; slightly more text than images |
| **Optimise for mobile** | 320–480px width; compress images (<1MB); test on small screens |
| **Alt text** | Describe images for accessibility |
| **Dark/light mode** | Check readability in both; test before sending |

### Using AI for Emails

Use the T-C-R-E-I framework from [[04 - Create Engaging Content#3. Using AI for Content Creation]]. AI can generate subject line variations, draft email copy, and suggest send times. Always review AI output for accuracy and brand voice.

### Email Essentials

**Footer:** Unsubscribe link (one-click), physical address (CAN-SPAM requirement), contact info, social links, preference management centre. Supports compliance and trust.

## 3. Managing Successful Campaigns

### A/B Testing

| Element | Action |
|---------|--------|
| **Method** | Two versions, one varying element; send to halves or 25%/25%/50% (test halves, winner to remainder) |
| **What to test** | Subject lines, send times, CTAs, images, content length, personalisation |
| **Measure** | Open rate, click rate, conversion rate—depending on what you're testing |

### Landing Pages

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Relevant** | Link to the page that matches email content—not just the homepage |
| **Reliable** | Works on mobile, laptop, tablet |
| **Accessible** | Clear fonts, contrast, alt text, keyboard/screen reader support |
| **Fast** | Quick loading; optimised images |

### Segmentation Examples

| Segment | Benefit |
|---------|---------|
| **Location** | Event emails for local customers |
| **Purchase history** | Cross-sell and upsell based on what they've bought |
| **Engagement level** | Re-engage inactive subscribers; reward active ones |
| **Lifecycle stage** | Different content for new vs. loyal customers |

### Key Automation Flows

| Flow | Trigger | Goal |
|------|---------|------|
| **Welcome series** | New subscriber | Introduce brand, set expectations, first offer |
| **Cart abandonment** | Added to cart, didn't purchase | Recover lost sales (send within 1 hour, follow up at 24h and 72h) |
| **Browse abandonment** | Viewed products, didn't add to cart | Remind and encourage |
| **Post-purchase** | Completed purchase | Thank, cross-sell, request review |
| **Win-back** | Inactive for 60–90 days | Re-engage or clean list |

## 4. Measuring Success

### Key Metrics

| Metric | Formula | What It Shows |
|--------|---------|---------------|
| **Open rate** | Opens ÷ delivered | Subject line effectiveness |
| **CTR** | Clicks ÷ sent | Overall engagement; typically 2–5% |
| **CTOR** | Clicks ÷ opened | Content effectiveness |
| **Conversion rate** | Completed action ÷ delivered | Revenue impact |
| **Bounce rate** | Undelivered ÷ sent | List quality |
| **Unsubscribe rate** | Unsubscribes ÷ delivered | Content relevance; < 0.5% is healthy |
| **Revenue per email** | Revenue ÷ emails sent | Direct financial impact |

### Bounce Types

| Type | Cause | Action |
|------|-------|--------|
| **Soft** | Full inbox, size limit | Retry; remove after 3 consecutive soft bounces |
| **Hard** | Blocked or invalid address | Remove immediately |

### Deliverability Best Practices

- Authenticate your domain (SPF, DKIM, DMARC)
- Use double opt-in
- Maintain list hygiene (remove bounces and inactive)
- Monitor sender reputation
- Avoid spam trigger words in subject lines
- Comply with one-click unsubscribe requirements

## 5. Checklists

### Email Audit

- [ ] Open product/service emails; note relevance and engagement
- [ ] Identify why some don't resonate
- [ ] Use effective examples as inspiration

### Email Creation

- [ ] Define topic and targeted message
- [ ] Write subject line (< 50 chars) and preview text
- [ ] Note 1–2 products/services and how they relate
- [ ] List helpful links (product, blog)
- [ ] Test in mobile preview and dark mode

### Automation Setup

- [ ] Set up welcome series (3–5 emails)
- [ ] Create cart abandonment flow (1h, 24h, 72h)
- [ ] Plan post-purchase follow-up (thank you, review request, cross-sell)
- [ ] Configure win-back sequence for inactive subscribers

### Deliverability

- [ ] Verify SPF, DKIM, and DMARC are configured
- [ ] Enable double opt-in
- [ ] Enable one-click unsubscribe
- [ ] Schedule regular list cleaning (monthly)
